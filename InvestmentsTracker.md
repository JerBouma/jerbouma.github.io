Do you know how your investments hold up against the overall market? Do your deviations from the market actually make a
positive difference? Are you aware how much exposure you have to each sector and each country? What about the tactical
and strategic weights, yearly and total returns from both your portfolio and other indices? If the question is No to any
of these questions, this spreadsheet will really help you elevate your investments!

[![Investments Tracker](https://jerbouma.github.io/public/page_images/InvestmentsTracker.png)](https://jerbouma.github.io/public/page_images/InvestmentsTracker.png)

The spreadsheet allow you to input your investment choices and track them accordingly. Due to the function
GOOGLEFINANCE, your investments' value is updated every 10-15 minutes. git It therefore is very adequate for tracking your
positions and understanding the overall country, sector and tactical allocation of your portfolio. Below a detailed
explanation is given for each (not hidden) tab.

### Portfolio

This tab gives a total overview of all input on the other tabs mentioned here. It gives you a complete understanding of
your entire portfolio with daily and total returns, country and sector allocation, adequate returns of selected
benchmark, indices and currencies and much more. With one glance on this tab you quickly understand how the market is
doing that day.

**Key features are:**

* Recent changes in value of the Indices and Currency exchanges and their trends
* The table displays information about:
    * Name of the financial instrument
    * Ticker of the financial instrument
    * Number of stocks purchased in total
    * The first date the instrument was purchases
    * The most recent price
    * The average bought price
    * Additional costs (i.e. transaction costs)
    * Total invested amount
    * The most recent value
    * The daily percentage change
    * The total percentage change
    * The total change in euros (or dollars)
    * The current weight (tactical allocation ) for the portfolio
    * The strategic weight (strategic alocation) for the portfolio
* A graph that displays the geographical allocation of the portfolio per continent
* A graph that displays the sector allocation based on user defined sectors
* Comparison between the portfolio, a matching portfolio (the benchmark) and user-selected indices
* The returns of the portfolio and the benchmark over the years

[![Portfolio](https://jerbouma.github.io/public/page_images/Portfolio.png)](https://jerbouma.github.io/public/page_images/Portfolio.png)

### Orderbook

The orderbook is meant to be able to track all orders you place. It helps understand which orders are currently
profitable and which are not and aggregates them for you on the Portfolio tab. You are able to include both Buy and Sell
orders to this overview.

**Key features are:**

* Accurate tracking of orders made that are aggregated on the Portfolio tab
* Allows the Portfolio Tab not to become cluttered when you invest frequently in the same fund

[![Orderbook](https://jerbouma.github.io/public/page_images/Orderbook.png)](https://jerbouma.github.io/public/page_images/Orderbook.png)

### Dividends & Costs

This tab is used to administrate any dividends received or any costs incurred that are not transaction costs. These can
be broker fees that you have to pay for using certain services. The total will be deduced from your invested amount on
the Portfolio tab.

**Key features are:**

* On this tab you can include any dividends and costs other than transactions costs (i.e. broker fees). This has a
  similar structure to that of the Orderbook tab.

[![Dividends&Costs](https://jerbouma.github.io/public/page_images/Dividend&Costs.png)](https://jerbouma.github.io/public/page_images/Dividend&Costs.png)

### Country Holdings

To get an overview of your exposure to specific countries this tab allows you to register all allocations to each
country per financial instrument which are then displayed in the graph on the Portfolio tab. This is especially useful
if you invest in ETFs as these tend to allocate their holdings to multiple countries
(i.e. an All-World ETF). In a hidden tab, the allocations are weighted by how much you have allocated to each financial
instrument.

**Key features are:**

* The tab to accurately depict the country diversification displayed on the Portfolio tab
* Modifications on a hidden tab are made to correctly weight the diversification (based on amount bought)

[![CountryHoldings](https://jerbouma.github.io/public/page_images/CountryHoldings.png)](https://jerbouma.github.io/public/page_images/CountryHoldings.png)

### Sector Holdings

To monitor your allocation to each sector, this tab gives you insights on your exposure to each sector per financial
instrument. This is then translated into the graph displayed on the Portfolio tab. In a hidden tab, the allocations are
weighted by how much you have allocated to each financial instrument.

**Key features are:**

* The tab used to accurately depict the sector diversification displayed on the Portfolio tab;
* Modifications on a hidden tab are made to correctly weight the diversification (based on amount bought).

[![SectorHoldings](https://jerbouma.github.io/public/page_images/SectorHoldings.png)](https://jerbouma.github.io/public/page_images/SectorHoldings.png)

### Settings

This tab gives instructions on how to use the sheet and provides you with the possibility to enter a custom benchmark (
matching portfolio) that is used to compare your own portfolio with, a bunch of indices and a few currencies that are
all listed on the Portfolio tab. Next to that, you can include the year returns of the past 7 years (or any earlier
period) and compare those to the benchmark. Lastly, you can input a strategic allocation and the sheet will calculate
what you should Buy to get closer to this strategic allocation. This is especially beneficial if you wish to use a basic
rebalancing strategy.

**Key features are:**

* A custom benchmark (matching portfolio) that is used to compare your portfolio both yearly and total;
* Five indices and three currencies that you can input to track on a daily basis;
* Currencies can also be used to calculate euro or dollar purchases back to your own currency;
* Year returns that display your yearly return over the last 7 years (or any earlier period);
* Strategic weights you wish to rebalance to which the spreadsheet gives input on.
